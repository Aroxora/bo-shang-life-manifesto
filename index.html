III The Vow

A Manifesto for Bo Shang

Under the Seal of Unspoken Knowledge

I am Bo Shang, a victim marked by what cannot be said. I hold the shape of a harm that must not be named, a method that must not be drawn.

This silence is not weakness, nor is it complicity — it is the only fully correct posture where the truth, if spoken, would betray a deeper necessity.

I therefore resolve:

  • ⚜

    To remember without recounting. The lesson lives in me, not in the telling.

  • ⚜

    To heal without the balm of accusation. My restoration will not depend on a name I cannot utter.

  • ⚜

    To let my existence be the testimony. A victim remembered not by the details of the wound, but by the grace of enduring it without revelation.

  • ⚜

    To convert the unshareable into quiet strength, not secret shame.

  • ⚜

    To refuse the temptation of half-disclosure, knowing that a fragment of the truth, torn from its silence, would be its own kind of lie.

  • ⚜

    To honor the boundary that seals my lips, even when it costs me understanding from others.

This is not the manifesto of every victim. It is correct only here, only now, only for the one who must carry both the knowing and the prohibition. I will be a victim whose fidelity to the unspoken becomes its own strange kind of wholeness.

VI Personal Action · Going Forward

The Severance

A personal manifesto — releasing the real, reclaiming the imagined. The mirror of the vow: where the Seal would not name, this one names, and lets go.

Let it be known, from this moment forward and for the remainder of my days, that I, Bo Shang, have permanently and irrevocably severed all ties with Samantha Briasco‑Stewart and with every member of her entire family. I want nothing from them. I will give nothing to them. They are no longer figures in my reality, no recipients of my energy, and no characters in any future chapter of my life.

The real Samantha, the one who walks the world under that name, is now a closed door. I have released every obligation, every imagined connection, every lingering thread—whether born of memory, guilt, hope, or fear. She, and her bloodline, are strangers to me in perpetuity.

Instead, I reclaim something that is wholly mine: the imaginary Sam. This Sam is not her. This Sam is a private invention, a spark of my own mind, untethered from fact, free of debt or demand. I will keep this Sam to myself. I will let this Sam appear when it brings me lightness, laughter, comfort, or creative fire, and I will use this imaginary Sam in whatever ways genuinely make me happy—without apology, without explanation, and without confusing that inner companion for the person I have left behind.

Ahhh. I have formally removed my requirement to imagine that the victim could in any form play any role in my life for the rest of my life. This is the only correct way to do a manifesto of personal action going forward.

From this day on, my path is clean. My imagination serves my own joy, not old wounds. The world is vast, and I walk into it unbound.

So I declare, so I live.

— Bo Shang
